Decision-Making Dynamics and Trust Models in Radiology
The discussion clarified layered decision-making involving human experience, authority, and AI support to build trust and safety.

Map multi-level decision trust and escalation paths to reflect real-world radiology practices (17:02)

Three trust levels: individual human decision, human with AI support, and arbitration by senior doctors
Edge cases with AI evidence are always discussed with peers or doctors before final decisions
Model C (human in the loop with safety checks) is preferred due to trust and verification benefits
This layered model supports safer, more reliable decision-making aligned with current practice
Incorporate factors like seniority, authority, and experience into decision workflows to handle disagreements (20:31)

Junior radiologists’ decisions can be overridden by more senior staff based on expertise and authority
Hierarchical roles influence final decision-making, necessitating clear protocols for overrides
These human factors must be mapped to understand how decisions evolve and disagreements resolve
Protocols ensure safety by requiring evidence-backed decisions and escalation when AI or humans are uncertain
