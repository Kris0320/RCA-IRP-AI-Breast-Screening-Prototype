## What this snapshot contains
This snapshot documents two interview synthesis sections. Interview 1 is Hutan Ashrafian, with screenshots from a video call, an Imperial profile, and an interview overview about handling human-AI disagreement in AI-assisted breast cancer screening. Interview 2 is Professor Deborah Cunningham, with screenshots from the call, a profile slide, and an interview overview about AI integration in breast cancer screening, arbitration, workflow change, trust, responsibility, and AI-human disagreement.

## Why it matters to the IRP project
The snapshot connects expert interviews to the project's central research questions. It shows that both interviews were framed around human-AI disagreement, clinical responsibility, trust mechanisms, arbitration, and whether current NHS workflow can accommodate AI capabilities. This supports the evidence base for focusing the design intervention on disagreement handling rather than broad public-facing AI transparency.

## Extracted concepts
- Interview 1: Hutan Ashrafian.
- Topic: handling human-AI disagreement in AI-assisted breast cancer screening.
- Direction: medical AI, clinical responsibility, trust mechanisms.
- Core questions: what happens when AI judgement differs from radiologist judgement; who takes responsibility; how clinicians trust or challenge AI; whether current NHS workflow can accommodate AI capabilities.
- Interview 2: Professor Deborah Cunningham.
- Topic: AI integration in breast cancer screening.
- Focus areas: arbitration, workflow change, trust, responsibility, AI-human disagreement.
- Core focus: how NHS breast screening arbitration works, how AI might enter the reading workflow, and what challenges emerge when AI judgement differs from human radiologists.
