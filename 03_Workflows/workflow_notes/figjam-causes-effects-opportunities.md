## What this snapshot contains
This snapshot shows a causes, effects, and design opportunities map. The central statement reads: "Current breast screening workflows treat AI as another reader, but lack a structured mechanism to review, record, and learn from AI-human reader disagreement." Above it are effects such as AI value lost, arbitration workload increases, human accountability pressure remains, and learning opportunities are missed. Below it are causes grouped under AI role mismatch, arbitration workflow mismatch, trust and calibration gap, traceability and accountability gap, and data / technology unstability.

## Why it matters to the IRP project
This is one of the clearest synthesis artifacts for the IRP. It translates research evidence into a design problem: current workflows may add AI as another reader without adding the mechanisms needed to manage disagreement, accountability, trust calibration, and learning. It directly supports the project's direction toward a service mechanism for triggering, guiding, recording, and learning from AI-human disagreement.
