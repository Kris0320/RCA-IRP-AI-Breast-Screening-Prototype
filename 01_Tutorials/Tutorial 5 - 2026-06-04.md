## Tutor feedback
- The move from a universal disagreement pattern to a specific anchor workflow is in the right direction.
- The selected anchor must be grounded in the practical reality of the NHS / screening organisation: existing authority models, review groups, protocols, policies, training, and command structures.
- Page 4 / workflow mapping should show the as-is pilot situation and explicitly call out where disagreement happens, what impact it creates, and why the intervention is needed.
- Personas should not flatten all radiologists into one type. The project should show a hierarchy of experience and authority, such as general radiologist / junior reader vs senior consultant / arbitration authority.
- The service blueprint should make clear whether it is showing the current state, the proposed intervention, or the future state after intervention.
- A dashboard alone is not enough. The prototype should address disagreement as a people problem as well as a technology problem.
- The intervention should include people/process touchpoints such as protocols, review sessions, workshops, training, guided disagreement discussion, case audit, or learning mechanisms.
- The learning loop needs a clearer model: who learns, when they learn, whether review is mandatory or informal, and how learning improves future disagreement handling.
- The upcoming radiologist validation session should be used to test not only interface ideas, but also how radiologists want disagreement to be resolved and learned from.

## Decisions / implications

### Universal Disagreement Pattern Map
The universal map remains useful as an analytical frame, but Tutorial 5 reinforces that it should not become the main prototype. It should lead into a grounded system view that shows where disagreement appears in the chosen anchor workflow.

### Specific Anchor Model
The anchor remains **AI as second reader + human-led arbitration**, but it needs stronger organisational grounding. The model should show not only human judgement and AI output, but also the authority structure around arbitration and senior review.

### Specific Anchor Workflow / Human-AI Disagreement System Map
Page 4 should show the as-is / pilot moment more explicitly: post-scan case entry, human first read, AI second read, comparison, disagreement recognition, arbitration, final decision, and learning. It should also make the disagreement moment visually prominent and show who is involved in resolving it.

### Disagreement Handling Logic
The logic should expand from a clean sequence into a serviceable process: detect, classify, escalate, resolve, record and learn, with attention to people, roles, protocols, discussion, training, and review. The tutor specifically pushed for a stronger explanation of how disagreement is actually resolved.

### Prototype Direction
The prototype should not be framed as only a dashboard. It should become a broader disagreement support package combining a digital decision surface with human/process elements such as arbitration protocol, guided review, case record, learning workshop, training or audit loop.

### 中文总结
这次 tutorial 的核心转向是：项目不能停留在“模型图”或“dashboard 界面”层面，而需要把选定的 AI as second reader + human-led arbitration 放进真实医疗组织的运作逻辑里。导师认可我们从 Universal Disagreement Pattern Map 走向 Specific Anchor Model 的方向，但强调下一步要说明这个干预如何嵌入已有系统：谁有权判断，谁参与仲裁，已有 protocol / policy / training / expert review 如何运作，我们的服务到底利用或改变了系统中的哪一部分。

导师最重要的提醒是：AI-human disagreement 不只是技术界面问题，而是 people problem + process problem + system problem。Dashboard 可以作为一个 touchpoint，但不能成为完整答案。项目需要展示如何支持人和人之间的分歧协商、仲裁讨论、角色权威、责任记录和学习反馈。Learning loop 也不能只是“收集数据”，而要说清楚谁学习、什么时候学习、是否强制、通过 workshop / audit / training / case review 等哪种形式学习，以及这些学习如何回到未来决策中。

因此，接下来的原型方向应该从“一个界面”扩展为“一个服务包”：包括数字化 comparison / disagreement surface、arbitration protocol、decision record、evidence pack、case audit、learning workshop 或 training loop。即将到来的 radiologist 测试也应不仅测试界面是否清楚，还要测试放射科医生希望如何解决分歧、如何记录理由、如何从 AI-human disagreement 中学习。

### English summary
Tutorial 5 pushed the project from model clarity toward operational grounding. The tutor agreed that the project is moving in the right direction, but stressed that the intervention must be situated within an existing healthcare organisation, with actors, hierarchy, authority, protocols, training, review structures and information flows.

The key warning was that the prototype should not become just a dashboard. AI-human disagreement is also a human and organisational problem. The service proposition should therefore include people/process elements such as arbitration protocols, guided disagreement discussion, case review, training, workshops, audit and learning loops.

For the next stage, the team should revise Page 4 and the prototype direction to show how the selected anchor workflow operates in practice, where disagreement happens, who resolves it, what is recorded, and how learning feeds back into future decisions.
