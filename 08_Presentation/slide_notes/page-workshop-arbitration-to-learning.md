# From Arbitration to Learning

## Source visual

![[page-workshop-arbitration-to-learning.svg]]

## Slide purpose

Show how arbitration supports the learning loop in the Disagreement Care Service Package.

This slide explains how one difficult AI-human disagreement case can move from case-level resolution into shared organisational learning.

## Key message

Arbitration is not the end of the service. It is the gateway into learning.

The final decision matters, but the rationale, disagreement pattern and evidence trail are also valuable. They can help teams calibrate judgement, improve handover, refine protocols and raise governance questions.

## Visual flow

1. AI-human disagreement detected  
Human judgement and AI output do not align.

2. Arbitration / senior review  
Evidence is compared and final decision is made.

3. Non-blame Decision Receipt  
Rationale is captured without blaming one person.

4. Learning Memory Card  
Case becomes reviewable and searchable.

5. Disagreement Review Workshop  
Team discusses patterns and learning tags.

6. Updated practice / calibration  
Protocol, training or governance questions are refined.

The loop arrow back to disagreement detection shows that learning should improve future judgement and escalation.

## Speaker notes draft

This slide shows why the prototype is a service package rather than a single interface.

In a screen-only framing, the work might stop once disagreement is flagged and arbitration produces a final decision. But in the service framing, that difficult decision becomes an input for future learning.

The Non-blame Decision Receipt captures the rationale without turning the case into individual blame. The Learning Memory Card makes the case reviewable and searchable. Then the Disagreement Review Workshop turns selected cases into shared calibration and service improvement. Over time, these reviews can refine the disagreement taxonomy, handover templates, training prompts, escalation thresholds and governance questions.
