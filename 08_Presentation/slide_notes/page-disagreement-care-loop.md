# Disagreement Care Loop

## Source visual

![[page-disagreement-care-loop.svg]]

## Slide purpose

Show that the project is not just a set of screens. It is a service loop that helps teams detect disagreement, preserve context, review evidence, record rationale and learn from difficult cases.

## Key message

Arbitration is not the end of the service. It is the gateway into learning.

The loop connects three prototype moments into one service system:

- [[Disagreement Detection Cue]]
- [[Arbitration Handover & Review]]
- [[Decision Receipt & Learning Memory]]

## Service loop

1. Human judgement recorded  
Independent human judgement is captured before comparison.

2. AI evidence layer appears  
AI raises a recall concern or suspicious region.

3. Disagreement Detection Cue  
Second interpretation differs. Comparison review available.

4. Warm Handover + Arbitration Support Layer  
Context travels with the case inside the existing review workspace. Senior review remains human-led and the support layer captures arbitration rationale.

5. Non-blame Decision Receipt  
Final recall decision and arbitration rationale are preserved without blaming one person.

6. Learning Memory + Calibration  
Difficult cases become learning memory, workshop material and future calibration.

The loop back from Step 6 to Step 1 shows that updated practice improves future judgement and escalation.

## Speaker notes draft

This slide connects the three prototype moments into one service system.

The workflow starts before AI-human comparison: human judgement is recorded, then the AI evidence layer appears. When the two do not align, the Disagreement Detection Cue makes the disagreement visible without turning it into an alarm. The Warm Handover and Arbitration Support Layer then preserve context inside the existing review workspace and help the senior reviewer record arbitration rationale.

The final recall decision is not the end of the service. The Non-blame Decision Receipt preserves the decision and rationale. The Learning Memory then turns difficult cases into workshop material and calibration prompts. Over time, this learning loop can improve future judgement, escalation and service practice.
