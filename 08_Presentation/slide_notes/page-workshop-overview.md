# Disagreement Review Workshop

## Source visual

![[page-workshop-overview.svg]]

## Slide purpose

Explain the Disagreement Review Workshop as a recurring, non-blame calibration session within the wider Disagreement Care Service Package.

The slide should make clear what the workshop is, who joins, what materials are used, and what happens after selected AI-human disagreement cases are reviewed.

## Key message

Arbitration resolves the individual case. The workshop helps the team learn from how difficult AI-human disagreements were resolved.

This is not a training lecture. It is a recurring review ritual that turns arbitration records into shared calibration, protocol improvement and governance questions.

## Visual explanation

The central table represents the review session:

- Non-blame case review
- 4-8 people
- 30-45 minutes
- monthly or bi-weekly

The four surrounding groups explain the service system around the session:

- Participants: who joins the review.
- Inputs: what feeds the session.
- Workshop artefacts: what makes the conversation structured.
- Outputs: what the service learns or changes afterwards.

## Speaker notes draft

This slide moves the prototype beyond the screen and into a service ritual.

In the selected AI-as-second-reader model, difficult cases may already go through arbitration or senior review. But if the reasoning stays inside one decision record, the wider team may not learn from it. The Disagreement Review Workshop creates a recurring, non-blame moment where selected cases are reviewed as learning material.

The workshop is fed by the Non-blame Decision Receipt, Learning Memory Card and selected arbitration cases. It brings together radiologists, senior arbitrators, trainees, audit or governance leads, and optionally an AI implementation lead. The aim is not to criticise individual judgement. The aim is to identify what made the case difficult, how the human and AI evidence differed, and whether the service needs better tags, handover, training material, protocol updates or governance questions.
