## 1-Minute Opening Script

Today we are presenting the current status of our IRP project on AI-human disagreement in breast screening.

Our focus has become clearer: we are not redesigning a full radiology platform. We are designing a lightweight support layer for difficult AI-human cases, especially where the AI risk signal and radiologist judgement need careful comparison.

Since the last stage, we have mapped workflow patterns, developed our disagreement models, built prototype touchpoints and tested the logic with Professor Sophia Zackrisson, a senior breast imaging expert. Her feedback was important because it challenged us to reduce visible steps and embed support into existing reading and consensus workflows.

So the project has shifted from a visible service toolkit toward a lighter clinical support layer: soft comparison cue, low-burden review support, non-blame decision receipt and outcome-linked learning. In this tutorial, we want to show that shift, explain our refined prototype storyline and check how well our plan connects to the IRP Learning Outcomes.
