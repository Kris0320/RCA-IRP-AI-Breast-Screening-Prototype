# Interview 03 Evidence — Sophia Zackrisson

Transcript note: evidence is based on an automatic bilingual transcript. Use paraphrases unless checked against the original audio.

## Evidence 01 — Workflow variation

- Raw / paraphrased evidence: Sophia described variation across centres and systems. AI can be implemented at different points in the screening chain, and existing double-reading / consensus practices also differ by centre.
- Why it matters: The project should not claim one universal AI-assisted breast screening workflow.
- Design implication: Frame the prototype as a support layer for difficult AI-human cases across workflow variants.
- Related prototype area: Universal pattern map; support layer positioning.

## Evidence 02 — AI as second reader / triage / risk stratification

- Raw / paraphrased evidence: AI may replace or act as a second reader, or it may triage mammograms into single or double reading based on AI risk score.
- Why it matters: AI placement changes where disagreement appears and who sees the AI score.
- Design implication: Keep the prototype modular enough to support second-reader, triage and consensus-support configurations.
- Related prototype area: Soft cue; comparison review; consensus review support layer.

## Evidence 03 — Consensus review remains important

- Raw / paraphrased evidence: Discrepant cases can go to a consensus list where another set of radiologists review the images and decide whether recall is needed.
- Why it matters: Human-led review remains central even when AI participates in the workflow.
- Design implication: Rename or soften “arbitration” as consensus / difficult-case review where appropriate.
- Related prototype area: Warm handover; consensus review support layer; decision receipt.

## Evidence 04 — Prior image comparison

- Raw / paraphrased evidence: Prior mammograms, previous recalls, ultrasound and biopsy history can change how a high AI score is interpreted.
- Why it matters: AI-human disagreement is not just a current-image conflict. It often depends on patient history and longitudinal comparison.
- Design implication: Make prior image status and previous screening history visible in comparison review and handover summary.
- Related prototype area: Expanded comparison review; Warm Handover Summary; Calibration Case Card.

## Evidence 05 — Every second counts / reduce clicks

- Raw / paraphrased evidence: Screening reading is high-throughput. Sophia repeatedly warned that extra prompts, clicks or classification tasks would undermine the workflow.
- Why it matters: A service prototype can show logic, but the clinical service must reduce interaction friction.
- Design implication: Remove unnecessary visible steps and make interactions confirm / edit rather than manual-entry heavy.
- Related prototype area: All touchpoints; especially reasoning capture and case library entry.

## Evidence 06 — Low-burden reasoning capture

- Raw / paraphrased evidence: Radiologists often record simplified reasons rather than long explanations. A system could suggest a rationale that the reader confirms or edits.
- Why it matters: Detailed rationale is useful for learning and audit, but it must not become a large extra task.
- Design implication: Use suggested reason tags, auto-captured evidence and short confirmation fields.
- Related prototype area: Non-blame Decision Receipt; consensus review support layer.

## Evidence 07 — Outcome-linked learning

- Raw / paraphrased evidence: Learning value depends on later follow-up and outcomes. Recall, ultrasound, biopsy or pathology can clarify whether the decision was clinically meaningful.
- Why it matters: Not every receipt should immediately become a calibration case or library entry.
- Design implication: Keep the receipt immediate, then run Learning Value Check only after outcome update.
- Related prototype area: Decision Receipt; Calibration Case Card; Disagreement Case Library.

## Evidence 08 — Existing audit / teaching culture

- Raw / paraphrased evidence: Screening practice already includes performance follow-up, missed cancer review, false positive reflection, teaching and case review practices.
- Why it matters: The prototype should connect to existing learning culture instead of inventing an isolated education product.
- Design implication: Position the case library as material for scheduled audit, teaching, interval cancer review or calibration sessions.
- Related prototype area: Disagreement Review Workshop; Disagreement Case Library.

## Evidence 09 — Downgrade break-space or wall-device ideas

- Raw / paraphrased evidence: Sophia questioned extra wall displays, tea-room devices or separate interactive tools, noting that breaks are breaks and radiologists work at clinical reading stations.
- Why it matters: Off-workstation interventions risk being irrelevant or intrusive.
- Design implication: Downgrade wall / tea-room / daily-space artefacts in the prototype hierarchy.
- Related prototype area: Soft layer toolkit; workshop / reflection material.

## Evidence 10 — Need to embed support in existing reading stations

- Raw / paraphrased evidence: Sophia said the smartest route would be to integrate support into the reading stations and the systems radiologists already use.
- Why it matters: Existing reading stations have the image quality, clinical context and workflow position needed for useful support.
- Design implication: Show prototype touchpoints as embedded overlays or support layers inside existing systems, not as separate dashboards or devices.
- Related prototype area: Touchpoint 1 expanded comparison review; consensus review support layer.
