## Relevance to our project
This report is highly relevant as a project-facing synthesis of UK and international AI breast screening workflow evidence. It directly supports the IRP's focus on AI placement, disagreement scenarios, arbitration, audit, and the difference between AI performance evidence and workflow implementation evidence.

## Key points for AI-assisted breast screening
- The report positions the UK context as cautious and evidence-building, with EDITH as a major prospective evaluation rather than routine rollout.
- It compares UK evidence with international projects including MASAI, PRAIM, ScreenTrustCAD, AI-STREAM, BreastScreen NSW, and Therapixel PACA.
- It emphasises that the key question is not only whether AI can detect lesions, but who sees AI, when they see it, whether they change their judgement, and how that change is recorded.
- It highlights prospective, silent deployment, shadow evaluation, risk stratification, decision referral, and AI-as-reader replacement as distinct implementation types.

## Key points for AI placement / workflow
- The report clearly distinguishes AI placement patterns including AI after first reader as a safety net, AI before readers as triage / risk stratification, AI as a reader replacement, and AI as a silent background system.
- It supports the project's decision to use a "Universal Disagreement Pattern Map" rather than "Universal Workflow".
- It gives comparative workflow examples where AI placement changes the location and type of disagreement.
- EDITH is described as especially important because it tests multiple AI intervention positions in one national screening pathway.

## Key points for disagreement / arbitration / accountability
- The report identifies multiple disagreement types: human-AI initial read disagreement, human-human double-read disagreement, AI triage threshold disagreement, safety-net acceptance/rejection disagreement, arbitration disagreement, lesion localisation disagreement, hindsight disagreement after follow-up, and calibration-drift pseudo-disagreement.
- It foregrounds the problem of "AI correct but not absorbed by the workflow" as a key design opportunity.
- It links disagreement to interval cancers, next-round cancers, arbitration interfaces, reader trust, and audit.
- It supports the need for a decision trace: who saw what, when, how disagreement was triggered, how it was resolved, and what later evidence showed.

## Possible evidence to cite later
- Strong support for the project's four-pattern placement framing.
- Useful comparative source for Page 2: shared screening backbone plus AI placement variations.
- Evidence vocabulary for naming different disagreement types.
- Support for focusing the service design intervention on comparison, mismatch detection, arbitration, audit trail, and learning loop.
