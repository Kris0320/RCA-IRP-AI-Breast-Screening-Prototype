# Artificial Intelligence for Breast Cancer Screening in Mammography (AI-STREAM)

## Source file
![[Artificial intelligence for breast cancer screening in mammography (AI-STREAM).pdf]]

## Source type
Paper

## Relevance to our project
This source supports the IRP by showing a prospective, real-world breast screening study where AI-CAD is inserted into a national single-reading workflow. It is useful evidence that AI can improve cancer detection without necessarily increasing recall, but it is less directly focused on arbitration because the study setting is not a UK double-reading / arbitration pathway.

## Key points for AI-assisted breast screening
- Prospective multicentre cohort study in South Korea's national breast cancer screening programme.
- Included 24,543 women and 140 screen-detected breast cancers.
- Breast radiologists using AI-CAD had a higher cancer detection rate than without AI-CAD: 5.70 per 1000 vs 5.01 per 1000, a reported 13.8% increase.
- Recall rates for breast radiologists were not significantly different with vs without AI-CAD.
- AI-CAD was used to provide abnormality scores and visual outputs, supporting radiologist interpretation rather than replacing final clinical responsibility.

## Key points for AI placement / workflow
- The workflow is a sequential reveal model: radiologists first interpreted without AI-CAD, then AI-CAD results were presented and a second interpretation was recorded.
- The first interpretation could not be modified after AI-CAD review, making the source useful for studying how AI changes judgement after an initial human read.
- The study is situated in a single-reading strategy, unlike the UK / NHS double-reading model.
- The system used a cloud-based imaging data management platform and a commercial AI-CAD system.

## Key points for disagreement / arbitration / accountability
- The source is not primarily about arbitration, but it creates a useful comparison point for "within-reader disagreement": before-AI judgement vs after-AI judgement.
- AI output appears as assistance, scoring, and marking rather than as a final decision-maker.
- Because recall decisions were still radiologist decisions, the paper supports the project's language of "AI output" or "AI evidence" rather than "AI decision".
- The paper does not directly address legal accountability or dispute resolution between AI and human readers.

## Possible evidence to cite later
- Prospective evidence that AI-CAD may improve cancer detection in screening without a significant increase in recall rate for breast radiologists.
- Example of AI placement after an initial unaided human read.
- Example of AI output as abnormality score / mark, not autonomous final decision.
- Useful contrast case for the [[Universal Disagreement Pattern Map]] because this is not an NHS double-reading arbitration workflow.

## Limitations / caution
This source is not NHS-specific and is based on South Korea's national screening context. It is not directly about AI-human arbitration, senior review, or responsibility after disagreement. The study reports preliminary results, and its relevance to the IRP should be framed as workflow comparison rather than direct evidence for NHS implementation.
