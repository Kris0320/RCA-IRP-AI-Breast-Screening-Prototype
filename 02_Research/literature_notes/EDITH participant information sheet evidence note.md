## Source type
Protocol / pilot document

## Relevance to our project
This source is highly relevant because it describes a UK breast screening study testing different ways of using AI within the screening pathway. It supports the IRP's decision to compare AI placement models rather than imply a single universal NHS workflow.

## Key points for AI-assisted breast screening
- EDITH tests whether AI can support NHS experts in looking for cancer in mammograms.
- The participant information sheet says AI has been trained on many mammograms and has been tested as comparable to a human expert at finding cancer.
- The study plans to include 660,000 women from approximately 30 breast screening centres across England, Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland.
- Outcomes include number, size and type of cancers found, recall, interval cancers, NHS workload, and acceptability for women and the screening workforce.
- Final cancer detection decisions remain human-led.

## Key points for AI placement / workflow
- EDITH has three groups: standard breast screening with two NHS experts, plus two AI-assisted screening groups.
- One AI-assisted model uses AI after an NHS expert has looked, as a way to look for cancers the expert might have missed.
- Another AI-assisted model uses AI before NHS experts look, to classify high or low likelihood of cancer.
- Low-likelihood cases are checked by one NHS expert; high-likelihood cases are checked by two experts before recall decisions are made.
- The participant sheet explicitly says all images continue to be looked at by a human expert, with final decisions made by at least two human experts.

## Key points for disagreement / arbitration / accountability
- The document does not use a detailed arbitration vocabulary, but its two AI-assisted groups imply different disagreement moments: AI after expert review creates a safety-net mismatch; AI before expert review creates a risk-routing / threshold pathway.
- The source supports accountability language in which AI assists NHS experts but does not replace final human decision responsibility.
- It also supports the need for an audit trail because the study will compare outcomes, workload, recall, interval cancers, and acceptability across trial groups.
- The opt-out section raises consent and governance questions relevant to AI use in public screening.

## Possible evidence to cite later
- UK evidence that AI placement is being tested as multiple workflow models rather than one settled pathway.
- Clear statement that final decisions are made by at least two human experts.
- Evidence that workload, interval cancers, recall, patient acceptability, and workforce acceptability are part of the evaluation.
- Useful support for the project's Page 2 framing: shared screening backbone plus AI placement variations.

## Limitations / caution
This is a participant information sheet rather than a results paper, so it should be cited as protocol / public study information, not outcome evidence. It does not provide detailed interface, arbitration, or disagreement-handling mechanics. It needs follow-up with EDITH protocol/results sources when available.
