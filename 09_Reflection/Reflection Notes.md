## Reflection — Visible design logic vs invisible user burden

A key reflection from this expert testing is the tension between demonstrating service design logic and reducing user burden.

As designers, we often need to show multiple steps in a prototype to make the service intervention visible: cue, comparison, handover, review, receipt and learning loop. This helps communicate the design logic and the value of service design.

However, from the user’s perspective, especially in a high-throughput clinical workflow, the ideal experience should be much simpler and more automated. Radiologists do not need more visible steps, more cards or more manual classification. They need support that is integrated into existing systems, reduces extra work and only asks for confirmation when necessary.

This helped us realise that the prototype has two different roles. As a testing prototype, it can make the service logic explicit. But as a future clinical service, it must become lighter, more automated and less visible. The challenge is to find a balance between showing the value of the service design intervention and designing a workflow that reduces interaction friction and cognitive burden.
